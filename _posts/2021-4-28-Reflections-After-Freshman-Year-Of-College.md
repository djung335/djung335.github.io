Welcome to blog post #1! In this one, I plan to talk a bit about what I've learned about after freshman year of university during COVID-19, as well as some of my favorite experiences.

By the time this post comes out, I will have finished all my finals. After what seems like an extremely exhausting year, I want to share a few experiences I've gained. Although my initial college experience will be slightly different than others due to COVID-19, I still hope you can find something helpful out of this.

First of all, I'd encourage people to care less about grades. There are so many important things you can get out of the first year of college, like connections

The most important piece of advice I would give to any incoming freshmen is to be open to new experiences. Personally, I consider myself to be a more introverted and uptight person so this is something I struggled with.

Although there were heavy limitations on most activities due to COVID, I still had many fun experiences in Boston.

The highest on the list would probably be messing around in the snow during the winter. On the week snow first arrived in Boston, my friend and I decided to go to Krentzman Quad and make a snow sculpture of Pikachu. Afterwards, other random students arrived to mess around in the snow. One person started throwing snowballs randomly, and then it just turned into a massive snowball fight between people we didn't even know. It was a really great way to release stress and forget about schoolwork for a bit. In addition, the school looks beautiful with snow so I'd encourage people to at least go outside when it does start snowing.
